ROTATION
AND TRANSLATION

The concepts of rotation and translation play fundamental roles in understanding the movement of planets in the solar system. Rotation involves the planet's spin around its own axis, directly influencing the succession of day and night. On the other hand, translation corresponds to the planet's orbit around the Sun, thereby determining the duration of a year.

The mathematical description of these phenomena has evolved over centuries, refined by notable scholars such as Copernicus, Kepler, and Newton. Their contributions were crucial for consolidating the scientific understanding of celestial movements, providing a solid foundation for modern astronomy.
